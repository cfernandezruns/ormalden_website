Municipal Platform 2021

Ratified July 29th, 2021

This platform was created after several months of community outreach, polling, research, and internal discussion and debate. It represents our vision for a progressive future for Malden. We believe that our government should work for everyone, regardless of identity or class.

  1. Pass an ordinance that signing a new renter lease will trigger checking for lead pipes.
  2. Update the Malden Traffic Commission to handle all forms of transportation, not just car traffic.
  3. Establish an Inclusive Zoning Policy to add affordable units required by zoning for new developments.
  4. Create a licensing system for mega landlords owning more than 49 units. Tax these licensed owners at the commercial rate rather than the residential rate. Have licenses revoked if owners fail to upkeep their property to acceptable standards.
  5. Pass an ordinance that all city councilors with personal or familial connections to a given business sector must recuse themselves from legislating on said business area.
  6. Ensure that all city council votes be publicly disclosed and stored in a searchable recordkeeping system. Upgrade the city website to make it fully accessible with full text search for all public information.
  7. Expand the MPS budget to support the increase of substitute, paraprofessional, before-school, and after-school educator pay.
  8. Support the increase in hiring and retention of BIPOC educators and staff.
  9. Ensure that all city meetings, forums, and public information are accessible to all communities through high quality translation.
  10. Pass an ordinance that city and school employees shall not be misclassified as contractors in order to weaken their labor protections.
  11. Pass an ordinance that everyone drawing a paycheck from the city budget is paid a minimum rate of $15/hour.
  12. Pass a Wage Theft Ordinance to protect Malden workers from the most common type of theft.
  13. Pass an ordinance that new hires shall receive training about their workplace rights and the benefits of unionization.
  14. Support the creation of new parks and green spaces, like the Spot Pond Greenway and the Malden River Works.
  15. Protect existing green spaces from development, and support the planting of street trees to lessen the effect of urban heat islands.
  16. Require that new city buildings be compliant with industry standards for green, energy efficient construction.
  17. Establish and uphold a non-cooperation policy between the Malden Police Department and ICE. No collaboration.
  18. End qualified immunity for the Malden Police Department.
  19. Ban Malden Police Department from procuring surplus military equipment through the 1033 program.
  20. Create a 911-dispatchable team of experts to respond to interpersonal, drug use, or mental health emergencies, as an alternative to an armed police response.
  21. Implement Ranked Choice Voting for Malden elections.
  22. Establish a progressive property tax rate, with lower rates on occupier-owned residential properties and higher rates on rental properties.
